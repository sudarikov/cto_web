Technology Audit

Are you struggling with your current tech with implementation taking too long, or being too buggy? Are you struggling with the current team or perhaps are unsure if your current tech stack can see you to the next phase of growth? Or, are you simply going for raising funds and want to be sure where you stand on the technology and how investors are likely to view it?

As part of our Technology Audit we will review your infrastructure, architecture, code, development practices, testing methodologies, security and tools; along with related people and processes. We will also capture your vision for the future and analyse the suitability of current technology and infrastructure stacks in place. The final audit report, which is separate to the requirements report, will explain the current system, highlight potential issues and suggest solutions and future architecture and technologies that should be used to achieve your business goals.

Here is detailed view of areas we are going to look into:

  • Business Analysis: current pain points and future business goals
  • Technology Stack: languages, frameworks, tools and services
  • Application Architecture: frontend, backend, mobile apps, APIs, DevOps, performance and scalability
  • Code Assessment: coding style, maintainability, quality and documentation
  • Database Assessment: design, data structure, integrity, data volume and performance
  • Development Process: process assessment, task management, roadmap, Agile assessment, productivity, codebase management, quality assurance, building and deployment, documentation and knowledge base
  • Documentation: centralized wiki, application documentation, infrastructure documentation, specifications, technical employee on-boarding, company processes
  • Tools, Software and Services Utilised: uptime monitoring, performance monitoring, issue management, version control, continuous integration, IDEs and analytics
  • Infrastructure Architecture: hosting, network setup, server setup, performance and scalability
  • Backup and Recovery: backup policies, automation, backup/restore test, disaster recovery assessment
  • High Level Security: SSL check, encryption, server access, code assessment, application access and infrastructure security
  • Team Assessment: team structure, identify key roles, interviews with every engineer, engineer profiles, roles and responsibilities
  • Quality Assurance Assessment: general level of automation, tests execution time, feedback loop and more specifically - unit testing, integration testing, regression testing, functional testing, user acceptance testing
  • Cost Assessment: infrastructure cost, team cost, tools/services cost
